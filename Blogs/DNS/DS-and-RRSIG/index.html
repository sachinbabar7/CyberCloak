PART A: DS Records

What is DS Record?

DS (Delegation Signer) record is a crucial DNSSEC (Domain Name System Security Extensions) record used to secure a domain.

It sits in a parent zone (such as .com or .org) and holds a cryptographic hash of the child zone's Key Signing Key (KSK).

This creates an unbreakable "chain of trust" from the root of the internet down to your specific domain.

When a DNS resolver queries your website, it compares the DS record in the parent zone to your actual domain keys to ensure your DNS responses haven't been hijacked or forged by malicious actors

Components of a DS Record

  • Key Tag: Unique identifier for the DS record
  • Digest Type: SHA-256
  • Digest: Hash of the child zone's KSK
  • Algorithm: RSASHA256

How the Chain of Trust Works

  • Generation: When you enable DNSSEC, your domain generates cryptographic keys (a Zone Signing Key (ZSK) and a Key Signing Key (KSK)).
  • Hashing: A digest (hash) of your Key Signing Key is created.
  • Parent Publishing: You submit this digest to your domain registrar, who publishes it as a DS record in the parent zone registry.
  • Verification: When a DNS-aware resolver queries your domain, it looks at the parent's DS record. It then checks your domain's keys and locally hashes them. If the local hash matches the digest in the parent's DS record, the resolver trusts your domain's DNS records.
